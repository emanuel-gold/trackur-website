Every job search starts the same way. You apply to a few places, things seem manageable, and then suddenly you have fifteen tabs open and a spreadsheet that's getting out of hand.

I've been there. Multiple times.

## The spreadsheet problem

Spreadsheets are the default answer to "I need to track something." And for a while they work. But job applications have state — they move through stages, they have next steps, they need follow-ups. A flat grid of cells doesn't capture any of that naturally.

You end up with color-coded rows, conditional formatting rules you forget how they work, and a creeping sense that you're spending more time maintaining the spreadsheet than actually job hunting.

## The app problem

So you look for an app. And you find dozens of them. Most of them want a monthly subscription. Many of them have "AI-powered" features you didn't ask for — AI resume writers, AI interview coaches, AI cover letter generators. The tool that was supposed to simplify your life now wants to be your career advisor.

I didn't want a career advisor. I wanted a board where I could drag a card from "Applied" to "Interviewing" and get on with my day.

## What trackur is

trackur is a Kanban board for job applications. You add a job, it shows up as a card. You drag it to a new stage when things change. You can add a next step and a date if you want to track follow-ups. You can switch to a table view if that's more your speed. You can import and export CSV.

That's it. That's the whole product.

No AI. No gamification. No daily motivational quotes. No social features. No "you've got this!" pop-ups.

## Why it's free

I built this because I needed it and because I wanted to learn how to ship a real product. The hosting costs are minimal, and charging money for something this simple felt wrong. If you find it useful, that's genuinely all the motivation I need to keep working on it.
